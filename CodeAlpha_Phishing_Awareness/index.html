Why Phishing Awareness Matters

Every day, millions of phishing attacks target students, employees, and organizations worldwide. Cyber criminals use fake emails, malicious links, and social engineering tricks to steal sensitive information. Awareness training helps users identify threats early and protect themselves from cyber attacks.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, OTPs, or login credentials through fake emails, websites, or messages.

Email Phishing

Fake emails pretending to be from trusted organizations.

Website Spoofing

Fake login pages designed to steal user credentials.

SMS Phishing

Fraudulent text messages containing malicious links.

Identity Theft

Phishing can lead to stolen personal and financial information.

Financial Fraud

Attackers can misuse banking credentials and payment details.

Malware Infection

Malicious attachments may install ransomware or spyware.

How to Recognize Phishing Emails & Fake Websites

Check Sender Address

Verify whether the email address is official or suspicious.

Look for Urgency

Attackers create panic like “Your account will be blocked immediately!”.

Hover Before Clicking

Always inspect links before clicking to check the real URL.

Check HTTPS

Secure websites use HTTPS and trusted domains.

Common Warning Signs

  • Unexpected password reset requests
  • Misspelled company names or domains
  • Attachments with .exe or suspicious file formats
  • Messages demanding immediate action

    • Social Engineering Tactics

    Fear Tactics

    Messages claiming your account will be suspended or hacked.

    Fake Rewards

    Scammers offer prizes, cashback, or lottery winnings.

    Impersonation

    Attackers pretend to be banks, IT teams, or company executives.

    Emotional Manipulation

    Attackers exploit trust, fear, or excitement to manipulate users.

Best Practices to Stay Safe

  • Never share passwords or OTPs through email or messages.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Keep your operating system and browser updated.
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments.
  • Use antivirus and anti-phishing protection tools.
  • Verify requests directly with the organization before responding.

Real-World Example

Fake Bank Email Example

Subject: Immediate Action Required - Account Suspension Notice

Message: Your bank account will be suspended within 24 hours. Click below to verify your account details.

Suspicious Link: http://secure-bank-login-alert.xyz

  • Creates urgency and fear
  • Uses unofficial domain name
  • Requests sensitive information
